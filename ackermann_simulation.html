Ackermann Steering

Interactive simulation for a 4-wheel differential-drive robot

Key Insight: When a car turns, each wheel traces a circle of different radius — but all circles share the same center (the ICR). The inner front wheel must steer at a sharper angle than the outer wheel, and the inner rear wheel must spin slower than the outer. This is the Ackermann condition.

Why "Ackermann"?
Without Ackermann geometry, front wheels would fight each other — one would scrub sideways, wasting energy and wearing tyres. The trapezoid linkage ensures both front axle extensions intersect the rear axle line at the ICR.
